Once upon a time, there was a young boy who was given the job of watching over the village's sheep. Every day, he would take the sheep to the hillside to graze.
One day, the boy felt bored. He thought it would be fun to play a trick on the villagers. He ran down to the village, shouting, "Wolf! Wolf! A wolf is attacking the sheep!"
The villagers dropped their work and rushed up the hill to help the boy drive the wolf away. But when they arrived, they found no wolf. The boy laughed at them, "I fooled you all! I was only joking!"
The villagers were angry at being tricked, but they went back to their work. A few days later, the boy played the same trick again. "Wolf! Wolf!" he shouted. Again, the villagers ran up the hill to find no wolf, and the boy laughed at them even harder.
Then one day, a real wolf actually did come. The wolf was big and fierce, with sharp teeth and hungry eyes. The boy was terrified! "Wolf! Wolf!" he cried out, louder than ever before. "Please help! This time there really is a wolf!"
But this time, when the villagers heard the boy crying "wolf," they shook their heads and said, "He's trying to fool us again. We won't be tricked this time." No one came to help.
The boy watched helplessly as the wolf attacked his flock. Many sheep ran away in fear, and some were caught by the wolf. When the boy finally returned to the village that evening with the few remaining sheep, he was very sad.
"I learned my lesson," he told the villagers with tears in his eyes. "I'll never lie again. I see now that nobody believes a liar, even when they're telling the truth!"
Moral of the story: Always tell the truth. If you lie too often, people might not believe you even when you're telling the truth.

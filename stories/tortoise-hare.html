Once upon a time, in a beautiful forest, there lived a very fast hare and a very slow tortoise.

The hare was always boasting about how fast he could run. "I'm the fastest animal in the forest!" he would say. "No one can beat me in a race!"

One day, the tortoise got tired of hearing the hare's bragging. "I may be slow," said the tortoise, "but I can beat you in a race."

The hare laughed and laughed. "You? Beat me? That's the funniest thing I've ever heard!" he said. "Let's have a race then, and I'll show you how fast I am!"

The other animals gathered to watch the race. The fox was chosen to be the judge. "Ready, set, go!" he called.

The hare shot off like a rocket, leaving the tortoise far behind. Soon, the hare was so far ahead that he couldn't even see the tortoise anymore.

"This race is too easy," thought the hare. "I have plenty of time to take a little nap." So he found a shady spot under a tree and fell fast asleep.

Meanwhile, the tortoise kept plodding along, one slow step after another. He didn't stop, he didn't rest, he just kept moving forward.

After a while, the hare woke up. He stretched and yawned. "I should finish this race," he thought. But when he looked down the path, he saw the tortoise was almost at the finish line!

The hare ran as fast as he could, but it was too late. The tortoise had already crossed the finish line and won the race!

All the animals cheered for the tortoise. The hare hung his head in shame. "I should never have stopped to take a nap," he said.

The tortoise smiled. "Slow and steady wins the race," he said.

Moral: Slow and steady wins the race.

The End

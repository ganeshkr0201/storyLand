The Monkey and the Crocodile

6-12 years intermediate folk tale
In a beautiful forest by a river, there lived a clever monkey named Raktamukha. He spent his days playing in the trees and eating delicious fruits. One day, he met a crocodile named Karalamukha who lived in the river.
The monkey and the crocodile became good friends. Every day, the monkey would share sweet fruits from his tree with the crocodile. The crocodile would take some fruits home to his wife, who loved them very much.
One day, the crocodile's wife said, "If the fruits are so sweet, imagine how sweet the monkey's heart must be! Bring me his heart to eat." The crocodile was shocked but didn't want to upset his wife.
The next day, the crocodile invited the monkey to his home for dinner. "My wife wants to meet you," he said. The monkey agreed and climbed onto the crocodile's back for the ride across the river.
In the middle of the river, the crocodile said, "I'm sorry, my friend, but my wife wants to eat your heart." The clever monkey replied, "Oh no! I left my heart hanging on the tree. Let's go back and get it!"
The foolish crocodile believed him and swam back. As soon as they reached the shore, the monkey jumped onto his tree and said, "How silly of you to think I would carry my heart with me! Never trust a false friend!"
From that day on, the monkey never came down from his tree when the crocodile was around. He had learned to be careful about whom to trust.
Moral: Be wise in choosing your friends, and always be careful of those who might betray your trust.

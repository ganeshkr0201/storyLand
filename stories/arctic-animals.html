Snowflake was a young polar bear who lived in the Arctic. His fur was as white as the snow, and his eyes sparkled like ice crystals. One winter morning, he woke up to find that his mother was gone. She had left to find food, as she often did.
"I'll go find her!" Snowflake said bravely. He put on his warmest scarf (made of sealskin) and set off across the ice. The wind was cold, but Snowflake's thick fur kept him warm.
As he walked, Snowflake met a friendly group of penguins. "Have you seen my mother?" he asked. The penguins hadn't seen her, but they offered to help him search. Together, they waddled across the snowy landscape.
Next, they found a wise old seal sunbathing on an ice floe. "Your mother passed by earlier," the seal told them. "She was heading toward the fishing grounds." Snowflake and his penguin friends thanked the seal and continued their journey.
Finally, they spotted a large white figure in the distance. It was Snowflake's mother! She had caught plenty of fish and was already on her way back home. She was surprised but proud to see how brave her little cub had been.
The penguins celebrated with Snowflake and his mother, and they all shared a delicious fish dinner. From that day on, Snowflake knew he could be brave when he needed to be, but it was always better to have friends by your side.
